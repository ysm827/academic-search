# Humanities and Law Discipline Profile

## Use When

Use for humanities, history, philosophy, literature, arts, legal scholarship, case-law-oriented literature, and book-heavy topics.

## Platform Route

| Need | Preferred Sources |
|------|-------------------|
| Broad discovery | Google Scholar, OpenAlex, library catalogs |
| Books and chapters | Crossref, WorldCat/library catalogs where available |
| Humanities journals | JSTOR, Project MUSE, publisher pages |
| Law reviews / legal sources | HeinOnline, SSRN, court or official databases |
| Open access status | Unpaywall, institutional repositories |

## Query Expansion

- Include translated names, original-language names, work titles, period, region, and archival keywords.
- For law, include jurisdiction, statute/case names, doctrine names, and law review terms.
- Do not assume journal articles are always the primary source; books and chapters can be central.

## Ranking

1. Source relevance and primary/secondary source distinction.
2. Authoritative editions, presses, archives, or legal databases.
3. Recency only when the field requires current law or active debate.
4. Citation count is secondary and may be misleading.

## Output Fields

Include title, author, year, source type, publisher/venue, ISBN/ISSN/DOI where available, access status, and notes on whether the source is a book, chapter, article, primary source, or legal source.
